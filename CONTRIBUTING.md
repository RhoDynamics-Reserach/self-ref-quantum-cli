# 🧪 Contributing to RhoDynamics

Welcome, Researcher! We are thrilled that you’re interested in advancing the field of **Quantum Cognitive Grounding**. 

RhoDynamics is more than a library; it is an open-source laboratory for exploring the intersection of Quantum Mechanics and Large Language Models.

---

## 🏛️ How You Can Contribute

### 1. The Science (Metrics & Protocols)
Are you a physicist or a mathematician? We need your help to refine:
*   **Stability Metrics**: New formulas for $\zeta$ (Zeta) or $\chi^2$.
*   **Fusion Logic**: Improvements to the **Synergy Integral ($S_{int}$)** or new entanglement kernels.
*   **Noise Models**: Simulating environmental decoherence in RAG pipelines.

### 2. The Engine (Core & Adapters)
Are you an engineer? Help us build a faster, more robust bridge:
*   **AI Adapters**: Add support for new LLM providers or local model formats.
*   **Performance**: Optimize the Hilbert Manifold calculations using GPU/FPGA acceleration.
*   **CLI UX**: Improve the research terminal experience.

### 3. The Gallery (Gold Assets)
Have you "matured" a highly specialized agent on physical hardware? 
*   Export your agent using `export <AgentName>`.
*   Submit it to the [**Community Showcase**](./showcase/README.md).

---

## 🛠️ The Contribution Process

1.  **Fork** the repository and create your branch from `main`.
2.  **Research & Implement**: If adding a new feature, ensure it aligns with the [Roadmap](./ROADMAP.md).
3.  **Test**: Run `pytest` to ensure no regression in the mathematical core.
4.  **Submit PR**: Provide a clear description and, if possible, a brief scientific justification for changes to the math engine.

## 📜 Academic Integrity
By contributing code or agents, you agree that your work will be licensed under the **MIT License**. If your contribution is based on a specific academic paper besides the root manuscript (202603.1098), please include the citation in your PR.
